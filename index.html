Welcome Aghan Proverbs

Afghan Proverbs are a rich reflection of Afghanistan’s cultural and social heritage. Passed down through generations, these wise sayings capture everyday wisdom, moral lessons, and humor in simple yet powerful language. They are often used in conversations to advise, teach, or express emotions and are an essential part of Afghan oral traditions. Afghan proverbs provide valuable insight into the values, beliefs, and experiences of Afghan society.

Featured Proverbs

هر چه بکاری، همان را درو می‌کنی

You reap what you sow

هر که طلا را بساید، قیمتش معلوم شود

He who polishes gold reveals its true value

آب که از سر گذشت، چه یک وجب چه صد وجب

Once the water is over your head, it doesn’t matter how deep it is

مار گزیده از ریسمان سیاه و سفید می‌ترسد

One who has been bitten by a snake fears even a rope

گندم از گندم بروید، جو از جو

Wheat grows from wheat, barley from barley

چشم بینا کور شود، دل بینا نه

The seeing eye may go blind, but the seeing heart does not

اول خودت را راست کن، بعد مردم را

First straighten yourself, then others

از آسیا دو آرد بیرون نمی‌شود

You can't get two types of flour from one mill
